Snaplate — Privacy Notice

Last updated: 2026-06-06

Snaplate helps you understand the nutrition of your meals from a photo. This notice explains what data is processed and how, with particular attention to the difference between the free and paid tiers.

The short version

  • Free tier — analysis runs entirely on your iPhone. Your photos never leave your device. There is no account and no sign-in.
  • Paid tiers (Advanced, Premium) — to use a more capable model, your meal photo is sent to our server for analysis and then discarded. It is not stored and is never used to train any AI model.
  • We do not collect your name, email, or any account identifier.

What we process

On-device (all tiers)

Your scans, food items, and nutrition history are stored locally on your device. They are not uploaded to us.

Paid-tier image analysis

When you analyse a meal on a paid tier, the app sends the photo and a short analysis prompt to our backend on Amazon Web Services (AWS), which forwards it to Amazon Bedrock to run the model for your tier (Advanced: Meta Llama 4 Maverick; Premium: Anthropic Sonnet). Processing happens in the United States.

  • The image is held only in memory for the duration of the analysis and is not written to durable storage.
  • The image and prompt are not used to train models. (Amazon Bedrock does not use your inputs or outputs to train its models, and we do not retain them.)

Diagnostics & feedback (optional, all tiers)

If you submit feedback (e.g. thumbs up/down on a result), we send a small, anonymous event (rating, app version, device model, an item label) over an attested, signed channel. No photos are included.

Your choices

  • Stay on the free tier to keep all analysis on-device.
  • Manage or cancel your subscription anytime in the App Store; cancelling stops future remote processing.
  • Deleting the app removes locally stored scans from your device.

Data retention

  • Local scans: until you delete them or the app.
  • Paid-tier images/prompts: not retained (processed in memory, then discarded).
  • Subscription records: retained while the subscription is active and for the period required to handle renewals, refunds, and accounting.
